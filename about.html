About Me

My name is Marcin Walczak, and I'm currently an undergraduate English major in my junior year at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill.

As an entry-level technical writer, my goal is to convey what is necessary to the target market with the utmost clarity. Though this goal may seem simple, I make every effort to produce quality documentation, from research to grammatical consistency.

Thus far, I am proficient in HTML, CSS, Bootstrap 5, Microsoft Office and Javascript. I also have sufficient experience working with Git and Github repositories, and I am familiar with several style guides such as the Chicago, IBM, and AP style guides. However, technical writing demands that you are always learning new technology and terminology, so I am always eager and quick to learn something new, whether it be software or a specialized communication platform exclusive to one company. Research and adaptability are one of the first skills I acquired when I chose to be a technical writer, and it serves as the foundation for my writing.

Working both with large -scale open-source projects and individual software engineers, I have an understanding of how developer environments communicate and collaborate with each other through forums, branching, and one-to-one communication. Research constitutes a large part of the technical writing experience, and it is something I place great emphasis on in my work.

I have experience writing documentation for Open Source projects such as Fedora, as well as successful programs used within specialized industries.
